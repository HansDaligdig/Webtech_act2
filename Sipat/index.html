What is CAR?

Cordillera Administrative Region or CAR is a region in the northern part of the Philippines. It is comprised of the provinces of Abra, Apayao, Benguet, Ifugao, Kalinga, and Mountain Province and one highly urbanized city (HUC) known as Baguio. It is bordered by the Ilocos Region (Region I) and Cagayan Valley (Region II).

What is Sipat?

Sipat is a term used in the Cordillera that means an exchange of tokens. It is a part of the indigenous way of settling a disagreement known as “Bodong” as the initial stage of the process. Warring parties give items of importance to the leaders until it leads to a final agreement called the “Pagta.”

The Mount Data Peace Accord (CAR Sipat)

On September 13, 1986, the Mount Data Peace Accord, also known as the Sipat Agreement or simply Sipat, was a peace agreement formed by the Cordillera People's Liberation Army (CPLA) and the Philippine government. The group’s leader, Fr. Conrad Balweg, met with former president Corazon “Cory” Aquino at the Mount Data Hotel in Bauko, Mountain Province. This agreement led to the foundation of the Cordillera Administrative Region and through Proclamation No. 324, has declared the date to be a special holiday within the region.

History

April 7, 1986

Fr. Conrad Balweg and his group had separated from the Communist Party of the Philippines – New People’s Army (CPP-NPA) due to differing beliefs and goals; choosing to focus on the rights and protection of the Cordillera people and their traditions. With the support of the tribal elders, Balweg formed the Cordillera Bodong Administration - Cordillera People's Liberation Army (CBA-CPLA) or CPLA.

1986

September 13, 1986

Both the government and the CBA-CPLA signed the Mt. Data Peace Accord or Sipat (Cessation of Hostilities) with then-President Cory Aquino offering a Bible, rosary, and an Armalite as Fr. Balweg offered a shield, spear, head ax, and a bolo in exchange.

1986

July 15, 1987

Executive Order No. 220, s. 1987 was issued which paved the way for the creation and establishment of the Cordillera region and its designated provinces.

1987

September 23, 1987

Administrative Order No. 36 was issued to administer the departments and agencies of the national government to set up their offices in Baguio City.

1987

July 4, 2011

The Memorandum of Agreement between the Government of the Philippines and the CBA/CPLA towards the CPLA’s Final Disposition of Arms and Forces and its Transformation into a Potent Socio-Economic Unarmed Force (Closure Agreement) was signed by Presidential Adviser on the Peace Process Sec. Teresita Quintos Deles, CPLA Chairman Arsenio Humiding, CBA President Marcelina Bahatan, and CPLA commanders and members to document the CBA-CPLA struggle, promote economic reintegration and livelihood of CPLA members and development of communities with CBA-CPLA presence, transform the CBA-CPLA into a legal entity for socio-economic development, and promote inter-barangay economic development for the selected areas of Cordillera.

2011

August 18, 2023

Proclamation 324, s.2023 was signed by Executive Secretary Lucas Bersamin declaring September 13 as a special (non-working) holiday to commemorate the Sipat Agreement.
